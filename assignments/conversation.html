Map Conversation

Pick a map that someone else made and lead a short (5- to 10-minute) in-class conversation about it. This can be a map you like or dislike, is old or current, online or offline, etc. The only real restriction is that you cannot pick a map presented already in class, either by the instructor or a fellow classmate.

Specific questions you may want to ask include:

Design choices

  • Is the map's design effective?
  • Does the map follow cartographic conventions as you understand them?
  • How could the map's design be better?

Data

  • Are the map's data sources listed?
  • If the map's data was modified, is this described?
  • Do you trust the data the map contains?

Point of view

  • Does the map make a statement?
  • If so, could the statement have been make more effectively?

Grading

This assignment is meant to be a conversation starter, so as long as you submit a map and talk about it by the end of the semester you will receive full credit.

Submitting your work

Email me a link to the map or attach it if it is not online (eg, if you are scanning it from a book or another source). You'll be given in-class time to lead a conversation about the map in the order of submission.

Deadline

Since we need to wrap these up before final project presentations, the last people to present these need to do so by December 5. I may assign class sessions randomly to students if it looks like we will miss this deadline.
