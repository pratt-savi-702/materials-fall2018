Final Project

Due December 13 at midnight

Guidelines

The final project counts for 40% of your grade for this course. It is an opportunity to use the skills you have learned in this course in service of a theme you are interested in.

Pick a part of New York or some other part of the world and a mappable theme and make ten maps of that area and your theme.

All final projects must consist of the following:

  • a theme that ties your maps/analyses together
  • a number of maps around your theme
  • a 10 to 15-minute presentation

The maps you make should use a variety of the techniques covered in this class. For example, this could include:

  • One map with your selected area highlighted for geographic context (that is, to show the area in its regional context).
  • One basemap of your selected area highlighting points of interest.
  • One population density map of your selected area.
  • Two other maps based on census variables.
  • One map using geocoding.
  • Two maps using geoprocessing and/or spatial analysis.
  • Two other maps highlighting your theme.

For an idea of what previous final projects for this course look like, see the projects from summer 2016.

Submitting your work

The project is due December 13 at midnight.
